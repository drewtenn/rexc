# Part II - Control Flow

A straight-line program does the same thing every time. Real programs
choose, repeat, and respond to the values they hold. Part II covers the
two ways Rexy lets you do that.

Chapter 4 introduces the C-family side of Rexy's control flow: `if` and
`else` for choice, `while` and `for` for repetition, and `break` and
`continue` for loop control. You will use these whenever the condition
is a simple boolean test. Chapter 5 covers `match`, the structured form
of choice. `match` becomes essential the moment you have enums and
struct-shaped data, but it is a useful tool over plain integers and
characters from the start.

By the end of Part II, you will be able to write programs that branch on
conditions, iterate over a counter, and dispatch on the value of a piece
of data.
